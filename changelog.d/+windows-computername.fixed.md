Fixed the Windows computer-name hooks `GetComputerNameW`, `GetComputerNameExW`, and `gethostname` to respect the caller's buffer and the standard size-probe contract. A long remote pod `hostname` no longer makes .NET's `Environment.MachineName` throw, which broke clients such as `StackExchange.Redis` with a `RedisConnectionException`. It also no longer breaks outgoing `TLS`: `SChannel` queries the `NetBIOS` name with a fixed buffer during the handshake, and a too-long name made `AcquireCredentialsHandle` fail with `SEC_E_SECPKG_NOT_FOUND`, breaking `HTTPS` and `gRPC` to external services such as `GCP`.
